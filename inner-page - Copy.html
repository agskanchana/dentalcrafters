Everything You Need to Know About Dental Fillings in Oakville, Ontario

Cavities are one of the most common dental issues patients face, but with prompt and effective treatment, your smile can be fully restored. At Dental Crafters in Oakville, Ontario, we offer high-quality dental fillings designed to protect your teeth and preserve oral health. Whether you're dealing with minor tooth decay or trying to determine if you need more advanced care like a crown, understanding your options is the first step toward a healthy, confident smile.

What are dental fillings, and why might I need one?

Dental fillings are used to restore teeth that have been damaged by decay, small fractures, or wear. If a cavity is detected early, the decayed part of the tooth can be removed and filled with a durable material such as composite resin or amalgam. In Oakville, we often recommend tooth-coloured composite fillings for their strength and natural appearance, especially for visible front teeth.

How do I know if I need a dental filling or a more advanced treatment like a crown?

A filling is typically the best option if the decay or damage is limited to a small area. However, a crown may be recommended for full tooth restoration when a tooth has extensive decay, has undergone root canal treatment, or has significant structural damage. Crowns provide more coverage and protection than fillings and are available in various types, such as porcelain, ceramic, and metal, depending on your needs.

Is getting a dental filling painful? What should I expect during the procedure?

Getting a filling is a routine and virtually pain-free procedure. We begin by numbing the area around the tooth to ensure your comfort. The decayed portion of the tooth is then gently removed, and the space is filled and shaped to match your natural bite. The entire process usually takes under an hour and requires no downtime.

How long do dental fillings typically last with proper care?

With good oral hygiene and regular dental checkups, most dental fillings can last 7 to 15 years or even longer. Composite fillings may need to be replaced sooner than amalgam fillings, but they offer aesthetic benefits. Avoiding hard foods and not using your teeth as tools can and will help to extend the life of your fillings.

Can dental fillings fall out or get damaged? What should I do if that happens?

Yes, dental fillings can loosen, chip, or fall out over time—especially if the tooth sustains trauma or decay recurs underneath the filling. If this happens, contact our Oakville dental office right away. We will assess the damage and determine whether the filling can be replaced or if the tooth requires additional support, such as a crown.

Your dental health is too important to ignore. Whether you suspect a cavity or need expert advice on when you need a dental crown, Dental Crafters is here to help with advanced tooth restoration and personalized care.
